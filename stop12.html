The Original Pioneers

As early as 1818, fur trappers roamed these valleys in search of beaver. Men like Englishman Peter Ogden and Danish-born John Weber have given their names to some of Utah's major settlements and rivers. French-Canadian Etienne Provost gave his name to the Provo River and the city of Provo. He is believed to be the first white man to see the Great Salt Lake and narrowly survived an ambush by SNake (Shoshone) Indians near Utah Lake in 1824. Provost was considered by his contemporaries as one of the most knowledgeable, skillful, and successful of the mountain men and earned the nickname "The Man of the Mountains." Provost traveled through this area in 1825 on his way to a gathering of trappers and traders on Henry's Fork of the Green River, the first of many Mountain Men Rendezvouz.
