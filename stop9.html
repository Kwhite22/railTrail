Main Street Across America

Just visible on the hillside lies the past in the crumbling edge of the Old Lincoln Highway. Reduced to odd patches of pavement from New York to San Fransisco, the Lincoln Highway was the first transcontinental paved road: "A continuous, improved highway from the Atlantic to the Pacific, open to lawful traffic of all description without toll charges." With those words, the Lincoln Highway Association proclaimed its mission on July 1, 1913. The country was just beginning its love affair with the automobile in the Roaring 20s, and this road captured its imagination. As the first road across America, the Lincoln highway brought great prosperity to hundreds of cities, towns, and villages along the way. It affectionately became known as "The Main Street Across America." Most of the highway through Summit County was paved between 1927 and 1932.
