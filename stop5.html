Kimball's Hotel

One of the few remaining Overland Stage stops still stands three miles northwest of here, just north of interstate 80. The Kimball's Hotel was built of native sandstone in 1862 by William H. Kimball, the son of Mormon pioneer Heber C. Kimball. Mark Twain, Horace Greeley, and Walt Whitman were among the notables who stayed there. The hotel later served as a station for the Holladay Stage Line and then the Wells Fargo Express Company. In the 1920s the Lincoln Highway ran right past its front door. Since 1908 it has belonged to the Bitner family and still looks much as it did in the 1860s.

As you pass onto the nest stop, keep your eyes open for the rail art at the I-80 underpass.
